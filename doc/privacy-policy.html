Skip Wait - Bypass Timers & Countdowns

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 29, 2026

This privacy policy describes how the Skip Wait Chrome extension (“Extension”) handles information. The Extension bypasses countdown timers and skip-wait pages on supported link shorteners and file hosts.

  1. 1. Permissions

    The Extension requests the following Chrome permissions:

    • Scripting - Used to run bypass logic on supported wait and short-link pages.
    • Storage - Used only on your device to remember your global on/off setting and short-lived bypass progress (for example, which step of a multi-page shortener flow you are on). Nothing in storage is sent to the developer or third parties.
    • Web navigation - Used only to detect when you open certain v2links shorteners (such as shr2.link and nitro-link.com) before the page redirects away, so the Extension can send you to the verification step in time. It is not used to read your browsing history broadly.
    • Access to all URLs - Required so the Extension can run on wait pages and link shorteners where it provides bypass.

  2. 2. Data handling

    The Extension does not collect or transmit your personal data, browsing history, or page content to the developer or any third party. It does not use analytics, tracking, or telemetry.

    Chrome’s local storage is used only for your on/off preference and temporary bypass state on your device, as described under Permissions.

    On certain supported sites, the Extension may read or clear data stored by that site in your browser (for example, in that page’s localStorage) only to perform the bypass. That data is not sent to the developer or any third party.

  3. 3. Third parties

    The Extension does not send any data to the developer or to any third-party services. Any network requests made by the Extension are only to the current page or to the link shortener or file host you are using, as required to perform the bypass.

This policy may be updated. The current version applies to your use of the Extension.
